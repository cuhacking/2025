## Frequently Asked Questions

<Accordions>
<Accordion title="Who can contribute to the project?">
Everyone can contribute.

We know what it's like to grind for internships, and having to learn technologies through personal projects that will become abandonware.

Our goal is to have as many people contribute to the project as possible, regardless of age, location, and/or educational institution. Priority is provided to undergraduate students at [Carleton University](https://carleton.ca/).

Your contributions will be shipped to production and used by many people. We have many different technologies spanning multiple projects, and actively encourage you to browse and contribute to whichever area(s) you'd like.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How can I join the core team?">
  - We don't do traditional interviews and technical assessments.
  - We invite external contributors to join the core team on a case-by-case basis based on trust, proven reliability, and delivered results.
  - We are extremely selective, as we need to move fast and our review bandwidth is very limited.
    - All members of the core team are expected to take ownership over areas and operate independently.
  - Get started by checking the [project board](https://github.com/orgs/cuhacking/projects/4), and commenting on an issue you'd like to take on.
  - When opening pull requests, ensure to closely adhere to the outlined conventions to reduce review labor for the core team.
    - We won't review your code if you repeatedly make the same mistakes.
  - Attend meetings and working sessions to meet others, and stay up-to-date on initiatives.
</Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What can I learn by contributing to this project?">
We regularly operate in the following areas:

- Project Management
- UI/UX Design
- Front-End Development
- Back-End Development
- Tools & Infrastructure Development
- Automation Testing
- DevOps
- Cybersecurity
- Artificial Intelligence/Machine Learning

Read [Tech Stack](/tech-stack) to understand the technologies used.

If you have any proposals/initiatives, we heavily encourage you to bring your ideas forward and take the lead in implementing them.
</Accordion>
</Accordions>

Brand Identity and Illustrations are maintained by the Design Team. The UI/UX is driven by a Design System maintained in close collaboration between the Development Core Team and the Design Team.

## Core Team (Software Development)

The core team has direct push access to the repository. External contributors need to fork the repo first.

All code and documentation is open-source. Meetings and working sessions are also open for anyone in the community to join in on.

We follow the [Extreme Programming](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extreme_programming) model, which is an implementation of the [Agile Methodology](https://agilemanifesto.org/). We prioritize regularly rotating areas of ownership and being well-rounded in all areas of the codebase.
